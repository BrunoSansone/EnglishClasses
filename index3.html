Get involved in the environment!
There are many ways to help the environment. Here are three:

Action for Conservation/Penpont Project

It was reported in 2016 that populations of the UK’s most endangered species have decreased by two-thirds since 1970. The organisation Action for Conservation was created in 2012 with a goal that every young person in the UK should want to protect the natural world. In 2019, they began the Penpont Project in which 20 young people aged 12–17 manage a project to help the local wildlife in Wales. These volunteers work with farmers and local people to improve the environment in an area of 2,000 acres – that’s about 1500 football pitches!

Our Bright Future
This programme is actually 31 projects across the UK. Each one helps people aged 11–24 gain skills and experience which will improve their CVs and help with future employment while they will be helping their local environment. All over the UK, over 115,000 teenagers and young adults have participated in Our Bright Future Projects. There are many activities to choose from depending on what you are interested in, from creating digital products which help the environment to repurposing unused local spaces to benefit the local community. 

(Young People’s Trust for the Environment)
Created in 1982, this charity aims to help young people understand environmental issues such as climate change, pollution and deforestation better. Their website has got hundreds of free resources such as fun facts for kids (Did you know the Siberian tiger is the largest of all cats?), advanced information for teenagers and free lesson plans for teachers. In their news section, you can read about the latest environmental projects in the UK and find out who has won their most recent writing or video competition.
